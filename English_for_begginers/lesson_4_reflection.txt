
  Q1:When should we use an adjective ending in -ed or -ing?
	-Adjectives that end in -ed are used to describe how people feel:
	“He was surprised to find that he had been upgraded to first class.”
	-Adjectives that end in -ing are used to describe things and situations:
	“Being upgraded to first class is surprising.”

  Q2:What are comparetaive adjectives?
	Comparative adjectives are used to compare differences between the two objects
	they modify (larger, smaller, faster, higher). They are used in sentences where two
	nouns are compared.

///////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q3:What is the from of comparetaives?
	1 syllable adjectives:		add ‘-er’ to the adjective 
	2 syllables ending in ‘y’: 	remove ‘y’ and add ‘-ier’ 
	2 or more syllables:  		more + adjective 

///////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q4:What are superlative adjectives?
	Superlative adjectives are used to describe an object which is at the upper or lower
	limit of a quality (the tallest, the smallest, the fastest, the highest). They are used in
	sentences where a subject is compared to a group of objects.  

///////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q5:What is the form of superlatives ? 
	1 syllable adjectives:  	add ‘-est’ to the adjective 
	2 syllables ending in ‘y’: 	remove ‘y’ and add ‘-iest’ 
	2 or more syllables:  		most + adjective

  Q6:Talk about Limits.
	‘too’ means more than needed.
	Ex: 
	“The car is too big for the garage.” 
	“Footballers earn too much money.” 

///////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q7:Talk about restrictions?
	‘enough’ means sufficient. 
	Ex:
	“I don’t have enough time.” 
	“I have more than enough.” 

///////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q8:Can we use both Limits and Restrictions ?
	Yes, we can even use both for the same situation: 
	“The truck is too tall to go under the bridge.” 
	The bridge is too small for the truck to go under it.”
