

  Q1:When to use s/es on a present simple verb?
	s:  In third person. 
	es: In third person and to the end of the verb that has a sibilant sound – ss, ch, x, tch, sh, zz.

/////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////
  
  Q2:What is third person?
	The term third person refers to someone else, i.e., not the writer or a group including the writer (I, me, we, us) or 
	the writer's audience (you). Whenever you use a noun (as opposed to pronoun), it is in the third person.

/////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q3:What is the present continuous form?
	[am/is/are + present participle].

/////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q4:When to use present simple and present continuous?
	simple: Use the Simple Present to express the idea that an action is repeated or usual or Facts or Generalisations. 
	continuous: To express the idea that something is happening now, at this very moment or Longer Actions in Progress Now.

/////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q5:What are frequencies?
	 Adverbs to describe how frequently we do an activity.EX(always,sometimes,rearely etc...)

/////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q6:When to use stative and active verbs?
	Active Verbs: Are used when describing an action.
	Atative Aerbs: Are about liking or disliking something, or about a mental state and are only used in present simple.

/////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

  Q7:When to use passive constructions and how do you form them?
	When we want to emphasise the object or recipient of an active sentence.
	Form: 
	Recipient + verb ‘to be’ + past participle (V3) 
	**Past participles are the same as the past tense for regular verbs (+ed). 
	***Many verbs have irregular past participles which follow no rules must be learned. 

/////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////////

 EXTRA:What is an active sentence?
	In an active sentence, the subject performs the action of the verb.
	Ex:The dog ate all the biscuits.
	Passive Ex:The biscuits were eaten by the dog.
 
 EXTRA:What are participles?
	A participle is a word formed from a verb which can be used as an adjective.
	The two types of participles are the present participle (ending ing) and the past participle (usually ending -ed, -d, -t, -en, or -n).
